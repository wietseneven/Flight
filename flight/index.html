Tijani

Tijani is the recently elected governor of North Darfur. He grew up in Nyala, the son of a Rizegat father, and a Fur mother. He was a former Rebel fighting against the government, but his militia signed the Doha agreement in 2011 along with a few other armed groups. He was sidelined at first by the government, and called a traitor by other rebels who were still fighting. But recent negotiations between the goverment and rebels has put him in power. He vowed to bring peace and growth in Darfur, but he has little experience and has been investigated for corruption.

Kuku

Kuku is a 13 year old boy living in the Nuba mountains. The area has been at war since 2011, when government troops attacked the SPLA-N rebels in the region. He has grown up under a brutal government bombing campaign. Antanov cargo planes have dropped nearly 4000 bombs on civilian areas since 2012. While the local government attempts to offer education, slowly another generation of young people is being lost to war.

Salome

Salome is a young student activist from Eritrea. She, like many Eritreans, is fleeing the repressive regime in her country. The first Eritrean refugees arrived in Sudan in 1968, and over a 1000 refugees continue to cross the border to Sudan every month. Sudan is also a transit destination for many, on their migration journey. Women migrants face a particularly perilous journey in their search for safe havens.
